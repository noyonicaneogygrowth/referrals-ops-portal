The MechanicsProgramme 101

How the referrals programme works, start to finish.

Click To Collapse
▸ How a Referral Works
1
The referrer shares their referral link with someone who gets paid from abroad.
2
The referee signs up on Skydo using the link. The link connects the referral on its own. No link? The referral can still be reattributed later (see R2.1).
3
The referee finishes onboarding and KYC.
4
The referee receives their first international payment on Skydo.
5
The referrer gets an email with a Google form.
6
The referrer fills in their address in the form.
7
The gift ships and arrives in 5 to 7 days.
▸ Referrals Are Reattributed

Attribution can be fixed. If a referee signed up without the link, or under the wrong link, verify the claim and raise it (see R2.1). The referral is then moved to the right referrer.

The reward still follows the normal rules. Most important one: a referral made before the referrer's activation earns $30, not the gift (see R1.3).

▸ What the Referee Gets

$30 in their Skydo wallet. It's credited once they finish onboarding, and shows in the wallet right away.

▸ What the Referrer Gets

The gift of the campaign they referred under. It is sent after the referee receives their first payment.

▸ One Gift per Campaign
  • One gift per campaign (per month).
  • More than one referral in a month? The gift goes to the first successful one.
  • Every referral after that earns $30 in the wallet.
  • It resets next month, so the gift can be earned again.
▸ Which Campaign's Gift Applies

The gift is set by the campaign the customer referred under. It does not change when a new campaign starts. Referred in June, referee paid in July? The June gift applies. Check any customer's campaign in Metabase (map below).

▸ How the Metabase System Records It
  • A referral has three states: SIGNUP_COMPLETE → ONBOARDING_COMPLETE → ACHIEVED.
  • Onboarding date = the first entry in cc_account.
  • Activation date = the first successful payment (EXPORTER_SUCCESS in the transaction table).
  • Referred before activation = $30, not the gift. This one rule explains most "wrong reward" complaints (see R1.3).
▸ Tracking

Customers see every referral and its status in the referral rewards tab of their Skydo dashboard.

▸ Who Can Be Referred

Anyone who gets paid from abroad and will use Skydo for it. Someone just starting out and doesn't have the last few months' payment receipts? A signed contract with their foreign client works as proof instead.

Reward Swaps & Exceptions

4▾
Also logged aswants the campaign gift instead of $30wants $30 instead of the giftswap the rewardgift not useful for the customer
How it works
Yes, the reward can be swapped, case by case. Every swap follows the same path: reason, then approval, then TechOps updates the ledger, then the customer gets an email. TechOps makes the change on the referee's account, so the referee's exporter ID is needed from the start.
Handle it
  1. Ask the customer why they want the other reward. Approval depends on the reason (for example: the gift is not useful to them, or they got the campaign email and expected that gift).
  2. Collect both exporter IDs — the referrer's and the referee's. The referee's ID must be in your first message.
  3. Post in #referrals-queries with both IDs, the swap asked for, and the reason.
  4. Once approved, TechOps updates the referee's ledger (and the campaign ID if needed).
  5. Check the change in Metabase, then make sure the email to the customer goes out. This email is sent manually (see R6.1).
Real cases
Exporter ID 171186 wanted $30 because the coffee machine was of no use to him. Approved once the reason was shared, ledger updated on the referee's exporter ID 219204. Exporter ID 175899 wanted the coffee machine because he got the June campaign email. Approved, ledger updated on the referee's exporter ID 217131, email sent. Both threads lost half a day waiting for the referee's exporter ID. Collect it upfront.

How it works
A one-time exception can be approved when the miss is small and the case is genuine. If approved: the referee's $30 is removed from the ledger, the campaign ID is set to the gift campaign, and the form email is sent by hand.

  4. If approved: TechOps removes the $30 from the referee's ledger and changes the campaign ID.

Attribution & Reattribution

5▾
Also logged asreattribute the referralfriend signed up directlyreferral missing from the dashboardattribute this referraladd the referral code later
How it works
Referrals are reattributed. The link connects a referral on its own, and when the link was skipped or missed, the referral can still be attributed to the right referrer after a quick check. The reward then follows the normal rules, including the activation-timing rule in R1.3.
Handle it
  1. Confirm the claim: who referred whom, and how the referee signed up. Get the referee's account email so you can find them in exporter_user.
  2. Pull both exporter IDs from referral_relation and check whether a referral row already exists for the referee.
  3. Post the reattribution in #referrals-queries with both exporter IDs and the proof it is a genuine referral.
  4. Once attributed, check which reward applies (activation timing, see R1.3) and set the customer's expectation.
  5. Tell the customer what was done and what happens next. Do not promise a specific gift before the checks are done.
Real case
Exporter ID 96995 had his referral attributed after the fact. The reward was then verified against the ledger. Reattribution and reward check are two separate steps. Finish both.
Related: R1.3 R7.1 R2.5
Also logged aswrong referral code usedcredit went to the wrong referrerduplicate referral claim
How it works
The link used at sign-up decides the default credit. When a real mistake is verified (the referee confirms who actually referred them), the referral can be moved to the right referrer, same process as R2.1.
Handle it
  1. Check referral_relation: who is recorded as the referrer for this referee.
  2. If the record matches the claim, nothing to change. Share what the record shows.
  3. If a real mistake is verified, raise a reattribution with both exporter IDs, the referee's confirmation, and the proof.
  4. Keep both sides informed of the outcome.

How it works
A registered business is a separate entity, so a customer can refer their own registered business if it gets paid from abroad and will use Skydo. Referring their own second personal, freelancer, or sole proprietor account is not allowed. It is the same person. Even for a valid self-referral, the reward follows activation timing (see R1.3).
Handle it
  1. Ask what the second account is: a registered business, or another personal account.
  2. Registered business that gets paid from abroad: allowed. Treat it like any referral.
  3. Second personal account of the same person: explain it does not qualify.
  4. If the referral is valid but the reward looks wrong, run the R1.3 checks before escalating. Referred before activation = $30, even during a gift campaign.
Real case
Exporter ID 199071 referred his other account (exporter ID 210259) and expected the coffee machine. The account activated on 29 May and the referral came before activation, so $30 was correct. The data closed the query in one reply.

4▾

How it works
"Gift achieved" means the referral qualified. The usual blocker is the Google form. No form, no dispatch. Once the form is in, delivery takes 5 to 7 days.
Handle it
  1. Confirm the referral qualified ("gift achieved", or state ACHIEVED in Metabase).
  2. Check whether the customer got the email and filled the Google form. This is the blocker in most cases.
  3. Form missing? Confirm the email was really sent before resending. It is sent by hand in exception cases (see R6.1). Follow up by message or call in case it went to spam.
  4. Form is in? Delivery takes 5 to 7 days. Past that, move to the tracking steps in R3.2.

How it works
Every dispatched gift has a delivery ID and a delivery partner in the tracking sheet. No entry in the sheet for a gift that should have shipped? Then the dispatch itself needs checking, and that is an escalation with specifics.
Handle it

  3. Tracking exists: share the status and expected date with the customer.

The $30 Wallet Credit

3▾
Also logged as30 dollars not creditedreferee missing the wallet creditsigned up but no 30 USD
How it works
The $30 lands once the referee finishes onboarding. It then shows in their Skydo wallet. Onboarding not done means the credit is simply not due yet.
Handle it
  1. Confirm the sign-up is connected to a referral (check referral_relation; missing: see R2.1).
  2. Read the referral state. SIGNUP_COMPLETE means onboarding is still open. The credit lands at ONBOARDING_COMPLETE.
  3. Onboarding done but credit missing: check the reward ledger for the referee's exporter ID, then escalate with the ID if the ledger is empty.
Related: R2.1 R5.1
Also logged ascan the customer withdraw the 30 USDdoes the referral credit expirebalance lower than expected
How it works
The referral $30 is wallet credit. It goes against Skydo's flat fee on a payment the customer receives. It cannot be taken out as cash, and it does not expire. If a fee is smaller than the balance, the rest stays in the wallet for the next payment.
Handle it
  1. Withdrawal ask: explain it is fee credit used inside Skydo. It cannot be paid out as cash or converted to INR.
  2. Expiry ask: the referral $30 does not expire. Careful: promotional credits can expire, and those are a different thing (see R4.3).
  3. Balance looks low or "gone": check the wallet ledger. Usually the credit went against a fee, which is its job. Share the ledger entry.
Related: R4.3
Also logged aspromotional reward in the ledger20 dollar credit expiry emailcredit still on the dashboard after expiry
How it works
The reward ledger holds more than referral rewards. Promotional and retention credits belong to other teams' campaigns and follow their own rules, including expiry. Referral credits do not expire. Promotional ones can.
Handle it
  1. Open the reward ledger and read the credit's label. "Promotional reward" means it is not a referral credit.
  2. Route promotional credits to the campaign owner (growth or marketing), and keep them out of the referrals channel. They confirm expiry and whether it can still be used.
  3. Do not tell the customer the credit works on their next transaction until the campaign owner confirms.
  4. Ledger entry really is a referral credit and looks wrong? Escalate with the exporter ID and a screenshot.
Real case
Exporter ID 124320 had a $20 "promotional reward" in the ledger with an expiry email, still showing on the dashboard. It belonged to a growth campaign, and the campaign owner picked it up. Routing it right the first time saves a full day.

Qualification & Status

4▾
Also logged aswhat counts as the first paymentis there a minimum amountreferral states explained
How it works
A referral qualifies when the referee, connected to the referrer, finishes KYC and receives their first successful international payment on Skydo. No minimum amount. No deadline. In Metabase, the states run SIGNUP_COMPLETE → ONBOARDING_COMPLETE → ACHIEVED. Only a successful payment (EXPORTER_SUCCESS) counts. Failed, bounced, refunded, or off-Skydo payments do not.
Handle it
  1. Read the referral's state in referral_relation before answering any "where is my reward" question. The state names the open step.
  2. SIGNUP_COMPLETE: referee still onboarding. ONBOARDING_COMPLETE: waiting on the first successful payment. ACHIEVED: qualified, move to gift delivery (R3.1).
  3. Payment failed or refunded? Reassure the customer the referral is not lost. The next successful payment qualifies it.
Related: R3.1 R5.2
Also logged asreferee clearly paid but not qualifiedstatus stuck on pendingreferral not counting despite payment
How it works
Three checks settle this: the referral connection, the payment's success, and the account identity. If all three pass and the status is still wrong, it may need compliance input. Escalate that rather than guessing an answer.
Handle it
  1. Confirm the referral exists in referral_relation (missing: see R2.1).
  2. Confirm the payment is a successful Skydo payment: an EXPORTER_SUCCESS row on the referee's account. Pending, failed, or off-Skydo payments do not count.
  3. Confirm it is the same account that was referred, and not the referee's other account.
  4. All three pass? Escalate with both exporter IDs and the transaction reference. If compliance is involved, act on their input and never share the referee's account details with the referrer.
Related: R5.1 R5.3
Also logged asreferee failed verificationcompliance rejected the referralreferrer asking about the referee's account
How it works
A referral qualifies only when the referee receives a first payment, which needs a passed KYC. A rejected referee cannot receive a payment, so the referral cannot qualify. The referee's KYC outcome is private to their account. Never share it with the referrer.
Handle it
  1. Explain the qualification rule without revealing the referee's outcome or reasons.
  2. Encourage the referrer to refer others who get paid from abroad.
Related: R5.1
Also logged asonly credit for the second referralcustomer expected two giftsis the account flagged
How it works
One gift per campaign, by design. The first successful referral in a month earns the gift. Every one after that earns $30. It resets next month. Nothing is wrong with the account, and it is not an abuse flag. A real fraud flag is a compliance matter and goes to them, without sharing detection details.

  2. Reassure the customer it resets next month, when the gift can be earned again.

Emails & the Google Form

2▾
Also logged ascustomer has not received the google formwas the renewed email sentcampaign id changed but no email
How it works
When a customer is moved to another campaign's gift, the campaign ID is your checkpoint. Same ID as before: the renewed email has not been sent. New ID: the renewed email has been sent. One caution: this email is sent by hand. A fresh ID change does not send it on its own, so confirm the send before telling the customer to check their inbox.
Handle it
  1. Look up the exporter ID in referral_relation and check whether the campaign ID has changed from the previous one. Then confirm the corresponding reward in referral_campaign.
  2. ID unchanged from the previous campaign: the renewed email is not out yet. Ask for the change and the send in #referrals-queries. Share the transaction ID if TechOps asks.
  3. ID changed: the renewed email has been sent. Ask the customer to check inbox and spam first.
  4. Fresh change? Confirm someone actually sent the mail. In past cases the ID changed a day before anyone sent it. Do not assume.
  5. Close the loop: confirm the email landed and the form is filled.
Real cases
Exporter ID 206407: campaign ID moved from 16 to 23, and the open question was whether the form email had gone out. Exporter ID 205: the ID changed to 23 but the email never fired on its own. It was confirmed manual and sent by hand the next day. The ID check plus a send confirmation covers both.

Also logged asnotifications on the wrong emailcustomer never notified the referee qualifiedupdate the notification email
How it works
Referral and delivery updates go to the email on record: the account email in exporter_user, and the address given in the Google form for delivery updates. Most issues come down to which email is on record, or to a referral that has not qualified yet, so no email was due.
Handle it
  1. Pull the email from exporter_user (email_address) and compare it with the customer's current email and their form answer.
  2. Wrong or old email: fix the record, resend, and give a time for the update.
  3. Correct email but nothing sent: check whether the referral has qualified. Not qualified means no email was due (see R5.1).
  4. Correct email and qualified: ask them to check spam and promotions, confirm the mail was really sent (manual sends, see R6.1), and resend if needed.

Escalation & Tricky Conversations

2▾
Also logged aswhat to include in a referrals-queries postwho approves reward changeswhen to tag techops
How it works
Escalation is the last step, and a complete first message is what makes it fast. TechOps executes ledger and campaign ID changes after approval. Atishay and Noyonica come in only when this portal and the data have no answer.
Handle it
  1. Work the golden rules first: portal search, Metabase lookup, and the matching scenario card.
  2. Post in #referrals-queries with everything in one message: referrer exporter ID, referee exporter ID, the exact ask, the customer's reason, and screenshots or ledger entries.
  3. For reward or campaign changes, the referee's exporter ID goes in the first message, even before approval. TechOps makes the change on the referee's account.
  4. Tag TechOps only for execution (ledger or campaign ID changes), after the approval is on the thread.
  5. After execution: check the change in Metabase, confirm the email step (see R6.1), and tell the customer. A thread is done when the customer has confirmation, and not before.
Why the full message matters
In one week of channel history, three threads stalled half a day or more because the referee's exporter ID was missing from the first message. A complete first message turns a two-day thread into a two-hour one.
Related: R1.1 R6.1
Also logged asescalate to whoever runs referralscustomer did everything and got nothingthe process is too slow
How it works
Referral queries are owned and closed in CX. The programme is not luck-based: when the steps are done, the reward follows. So an unresolved case always traces back to one open step or a delivery issue. Resolve here, and go to the referrals lead only when the portal really has no answer.
Handle it
  1. Reassure the customer they are in the right place, and stay confident. Do not refuse the ask outright, and do not redirect by default.
  2. Check their referral in Metabase and name the exact open step: attribution, onboarding, first payment, form, or delivery.
  3. Qualified but nothing received? Treat it as a delivery matter and chase it (R3.1, R3.2).
  4. Lead with what happened, what you are doing, and when they will hear back. Never make them chase you.
